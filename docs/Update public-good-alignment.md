Public Good Alignment – EGTS

1. Principles

EGTS is designed as a public good for the Ethereum ecosystem. Alignment is achieved through:

· Open access – No permission required to read governance trust data.
· Transparency – All proofs and source code are publicly auditable.
· Neutrality – EGTS does not favor any particular DAO, L2, or governance model.
· Sustainability – Funding via optional grants and validator rewards, not extractive fees.

2. Public Good Metrics

EGTS publishes quarterly transparency reports covering:

· Number of active contributors and badges.
· Geographic diversity of validators.
· Cross‑chain adapters operational.
· Total slashed amounts and treasury usage.

These metrics are posted on‑chain and mirrored to IPFS.

3. Funding Model

EGTS core development and infrastructure are funded by:

· Ethereum Foundation grant (initial 2 years).
· Optional tipping – Dashboards may include a “support EGTS” button.
· Validator rewards – A small portion (10%) of validator rewards is diverted to a public goods treasury.
· Gitcoin grants – Periodic matching rounds.

No protocol fees are charged for reading or submitting proofs.

4. Governance of Public Goods Treasury

The treasury is controlled by an independent Public Goods Council (5 members elected by EGTS validators for 1‑year terms). Funds are used for:

· Security audits of new adapters.
· Bounty programs for proof‑of‑contribution.
· Educational materials and hackathons.

All spending is published on‑chain with a 30‑day delay to allow for challenges.

5. Anti‑Capture Safeguards

To prevent capture of EGTS by a single entity:

· No single entity may control > 20% of validator stake.
· Badge Authority roles require multi‑sig with signers from at least 3 different organizations.
· Governance proposals must have a 7‑day discussion period before voting.

6. Compliance with Ethereum Values

EGTS adheres to the Ethereum Philosophy document:

· Censorship resistance – Anyone can run a validator or indexer.
· Permissionlessness – No whitelist for badge issuance (except self‑issued badges).
· Credible neutrality – Rules apply equally to all participants.

7. Fork Handling

In case of an Ethereum hard fork, EGTS validators will follow the chain with the highest total slashed stake (a proxy for economic majority). The EGTS core contracts are deployed on both forks initially; the community converges on one within 7 days.

📘 EGTS Roadmap (Expanded v0.3 → v1.0)
Version: 0.2.x  
Status: Active Draft  
Purpose: Define the evolution of the Ethereum Governance Trust Surface (EGTS) from early draft to full standard  

---

1. Roadmap Overview

EGTS is designed to evolve through four major phases:

1. v0.3 — Schema Expansion & Validation Hardening  
2. v0.4 — Cross‑Ecosystem Integration & Role Registry  
3. v0.5 — Cryptographic Badges & Trust‑Surface Automation  
4. v1.0 — Full Standardization & Ecosystem Adoption

Each phase builds on the previous one, ensuring EGTS becomes a robust, interoperable, governance‑grade trust‑surface standard.

---

2. v0.3 — Schema Expansion & Validation Hardening

Goals
- Expand governance event schema  
- Add advanced validation rules  
- Improve trust‑signal metadata  
- Strengthen audit log structure  

Deliverables
- governance-event.schema.json v0.3  
- trust-signal.schema.json v0.3  
- audit-log.schema.json v0.3  
- Validation rule extensions:
  - lifecycle enforcement  
  - authority inheritance  
  - multi‑role validation  
- Additional example JSON files  
- Updated diagrams and documentation  

Outcome
EGTS becomes a complete, enforceable schema suite.

---

3. v0.4 — Cross‑Ecosystem Integration & Role Registry

Goals
- Enable cross‑DAO and cross‑L2 trust surfaces  
- Introduce a universal governance role registry  
- Support DID‑based contributor identity  

Deliverables
- EGTS Role Registry (spec + schema)  
- Cross‑ecosystem trust‑surface adapter spec  
- DID integration guidelines  
- Governance charter mapping templates  
- Integration examples for:
  - DAOs  
  - L2 ecosystems  
  - grant programs  

Outcome
EGTS becomes interoperable across governance systems.

---

4. v0.5 — Cryptographic Badges & Trust‑Surface Automation

Goals
- Introduce cryptographic trust badges  
- Automate trust‑signal generation  
- Add audit log hashing and lineage proofs  
- Provide reference implementations  

Deliverables
- Cryptographic badge spec  
- Badge authority schema  
- Trust‑surface automation scripts (reference only)  
- Audit log hashing standard  
- Integrity proof format  

Outcome
EGTS becomes automated, cryptographically verifiable, and production‑ready.

---

5. v1.0 — Full Standardization & Ecosystem Adoption

Goals
- Finalize EGTS as a governance‑grade standard  
- Publish stable schemas  
- Provide full documentation suite  
- Support ecosystem‑wide adoption  

Deliverables
- EGTS v1.0 Specification  
- Complete schema suite  
- Integration guides for:
  - DAOs  
  - L2 ecosystems  
  - public‑good systems  
  - grant programs  
- Governance charter alignment templates  
- Full audit log lifecycle documentation  
- Reference dashboards and visualizations  

Outcome
EGTS becomes a recognized, stable, ecosystem‑wide trust‑surface standard.

---

6. Long‑Term Vision (Post‑v1.0)

A. EGTS‑Native Governance Dashboards
Visual trust‑surface explorers for proposals, contributors, and audit logs.

B. Cross‑Chain Trust‑Surface Bridges
Portable legitimacy across L1, L2s, and app‑chains.

C. Public‑Good Governance Registry
A shared registry of validated governance actions across ecosystems.

D. EGTS‑Aligned Grant Infrastructure
Grant programs that require EGTS trust signals for:
- proposal legitimacy  
- milestone verification  
- contributor identity  
- auditability  

E. EGTS‑Driven Governance Analytics
Metrics for:
- governance integrity  
- validator performance  
- contributor legitimacy  
- ecosystem trust health

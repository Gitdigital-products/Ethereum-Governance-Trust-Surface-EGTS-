EGTS Adoption Playbook – Overview

1. Purpose of the Playbook

The Ethereum Governance Trust Surface (EGTS) Adoption Playbook provides structured, step‑by‑step guidance for integrating EGTS into diverse components of the Ethereum ecosystem. Each chapter targets a specific adoption context, including DAOs, layer‑2 networks, grant programs, public good systems, and cross‑ecosystem coordination. The playbook assumes familiarity with the EGTS Charter and core documentation.

2. Audience

· DAO operators seeking standardized governance tracking and verifiability.
· Layer‑2 developers implementing cross‑domain vote bridging and fast finality.
· Grant program administrators requiring transparent, auditable funding decisions.
· Public good maintainers (client teams, protocol stewards, EIP editors).
· Ecosystem alignment officers coordinating governance across multiple projects.

3. Playbook Structure

Chapter Focus
01 – DAO Integration Adding EGTS to existing or new DAOs (on‑chain and off‑chain).
02 – L2 Integration Enabling EGTS on rollups and sidechains with vote bridging.
03 – Grant Program Integration Making grant decisions transparent and composable.
04 – Public Good Systems Adapting EGTS for off‑chain, social‑layer governance.
05 – Cross‑Ecosystem Alignment Composable governance across multiple independent systems.
06 – Migration Paths Incremental adoption from legacy governance systems.
07 – Governance Maturity Model Progressive levels of EGTS integration (Observable to Federated).

4. Common Principles

All adoptions adhere to these principles:

· Registry First – Every governance action is recorded in the EGTS Registry.
· Verifiability – All artifacts are signed and content‑addressed (IPFS or equivalent).
· Minimal Overhead – Adopting EGTS does not require rewriting existing contracts unless desired.
· Opt‑in – EGTS is an additive trust surface, not a mandatory standard.

5. Adoption Levels Overview

The playbook recognizes three core levels (detailed in Chapter 07):

· Observable – Only publishing registry entries and events. Minimal process change.
· Interoperable – Full EGTS lifecycle plus cross‑system vote acceptance.
· Trust Surface – Complete alignment with EGTS Charter and active dispute resolution.

Each chapter provides specific actions for reaching each level.

6. Prerequisites

Before starting any adoption path, ensure:

· You have read the EGTS Charter (docs/egts-charter.md).
· You understand the governance lifecycle (governance-lifecycle-narrative.md).
· You have access to an Ethereum address that can sign on behalf of your system.
· A public archive (IPFS, Arweave, or HTTP with hash) is available for artifact storage.

7. How to Use This Playbook

8. Identify your component type (DAO, L2, grant program, public good, or cross‑ecosystem).
9. Read the corresponding chapter.
10. Complete the “Minimum Viable Adoption” checklist at the start of the chapter.
11. Progress to higher maturity levels as resources allow.
12. Submit your integration to the EGTS Registry of Adopters.

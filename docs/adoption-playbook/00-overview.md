EGTS Adoption Playbook – Overview

1. Purpose of the Playbook

The Ethereum Governance Trust Surface (EGTS) Adoption Playbook provides step‑by‑step guidance for integrating EGTS into diverse components of the Ethereum ecosystem. Each chapter targets a specific adoption context, from DAOs to L2 networks to grant programs. The playbook assumes familiarity with the EGTS Charter and core documentation.

2. Who Should Read This

· DAO operators seeking standardized governance tracking.
· L2 developers implementing cross‑domain vote bridges.
· Grant program administrators wanting verifiable funding decisions.
· Public good maintainers (e.g., client teams, protocol stewards).
· Ecosystem alignment officers coordinating between multiple projects.

3. Playbook Structure

4. Common Principles Across Chapters

All adoptions follow these principles:

· Registry First – Every governance action is recorded in the EGTS Registry.
· Verifiability – All artifacts are signed and content‑addressed.
· Minimal Overhead – Adopting EGTS should not require rewriting existing contracts unless desired.
· Opt‑in – EGTS is an additive trust surface, not a mandatory standard.

5. Adoption Levels

The playbook recognizes three adoption levels, defined in Chapter 07 (Governance Maturity Model):

· Observable – Only publishing registry entries and events.
· Interoperable – Full EGTS lifecycle plus cross‑system vote acceptance.
· Trust Surface – Complete alignment with charter and active dispute resolution.

Each chapter provides specific actions for reaching each level.

6. Prerequisites

Before starting any adoption path, ensure:

· You have read the EGTS Charter (docs/egts-charter.md).
· Your team understands the governance lifecycle (governance-lifecycle-narrative.md).
· You have access to an Ethereum address that can sign on behalf of your system.
· A public archive (IPFS, Arweave, or HTTP with hash) is available for artifact storage.

7. How to Use This Playbook

8. Identify your component type (DAO, L2, grant program, public good, or cross‑ecosystem).
9. Read the corresponding chapter.
10. Complete the “Minimum Viable Adoption” checklist at the start of the chapter.
11. Progress to higher maturity levels as resources allow.
12. Submit your integration to the EGTS Registry of Adopters.

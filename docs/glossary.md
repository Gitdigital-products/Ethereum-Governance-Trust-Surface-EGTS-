📘 EGTS Glossary (Spec‑Grade)
Version: 0.2.x  
Status: Active Draft  
Purpose: Define all core terms used in the Ethereum Governance Trust Surface (EGTS) specification  

---

A

Actor
The individual or entity performing a governance action.  
Actors must have a verifiable identity and a recognized role within the governance system.

Audit Log
An append‑only record of validated governance events and trust signals.  
Contains hash lineage, validator signatures, and audit metadata.

Authority Scope
The set of actions an actor is permitted to perform according to the governance charter.

---

C

Chain‑Agnostic
A design principle ensuring EGTS functions across Ethereum L1, L2s, app‑chains, and off‑chain governance systems.

Context
The justification, rationale, or situational metadata associated with a governance event.

Core Contributor
A contributor with execution authority derived from governance rules.  
Responsible for implementing approved proposals.

---

E

Ecosystem Integrator
An entity responsible for implementing EGTS within DAOs, protocols, or L2 ecosystems.

EGTS (Ethereum Governance Trust Surface)
A chain‑agnostic standard for representing governance events, trust signals, and audit logs.

Event Integrity
The correctness, completeness, and consistency of a governance event.

---

G

Governance Action
Any action taken within a governance system, such as creating a proposal, casting a vote, approving a change, or executing a decision.

Governance Event
A structured representation of a governance action, formatted according to the EGTS schema.

Governance Validator
A role responsible for validating governance events and producing trust signals.

---

I

Identity (Actor Identity)
A DID or equivalent identifier representing the actor performing a governance action.

Integrity Hash
A cryptographic hash representing the canonical form of a governance event or trust signal.

---

L

Legitimacy
The degree to which a governance action is valid, authorized, and properly validated.

Lifecycle Stage
The position of a proposal or governance object within its lifecycle (draft, vote, approval, execution, etc.).

---

P

Portable Trust Surface
A trust representation that can be used across DAOs, L2s, protocols, and public‑good systems.

Proposal Author
A contributor responsible for drafting and submitting governance proposals.

---

R

Role
A governance‑defined identity category that determines authority and responsibilities.

Role Verification
The process of confirming that an actor’s role matches the authority required for a governance action.

---

T

Trust Signal
A validated representation of a governance event, including validation metadata, validator identities, and integrity hash.

Trust‑Surface Maintainer
A role responsible for maintaining the EGTS specification, schemas, and validation logic.

Trust Surface
The complete set of validated governance events, trust signals, and audit logs that represent governance legitimacy.

---

V

Validation
The process of verifying identity, role, authority, event integrity, and contextual consistency.

Validation Method
The mechanism used to validate a governance event (multi‑sig, attestation, quorum, etc.).

---

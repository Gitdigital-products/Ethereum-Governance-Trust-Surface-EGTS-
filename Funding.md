# Funding

## Purpose
This document describes the overall funding model of the ApexOS project, including sources of revenue, allocation of funds, and transparency practices.

## Definitions
- **Funding**: Any monetary or in‑kind resource received by the project from external entities.
- **Restricted Funds**: Contributions designated for a specific purpose (e.g., security audit, documentation sprint).
- **Unrestricted Funds**: Contributions that may be used at the Core Team’s discretion for any project need.

## Tiers (if applicable)
Funding sources are categorized as:
- **Individual Donations**: Small recurring or one‑time gifts via GitHub Sponsors, Patreon, etc.
- **Sponsorships**: Organizational contributions at Bronze–Platinum levels.
- **Grants**: Non‑repayable funds from foundations or government programs.
- **Merchandise**: Revenue from selling ApexOS branded items (margins go to project).

## Benefits
- All funding sources receive public acknowledgment in the annual financial report.
- Grantors may be invited to present their work at project events.
- Merchandise buyers receive a digital thank‑you card.

## Requirements
- All funds must be received through the project’s fiscal host (Open Source Collective).
- Any funding over $10,000 must be accompanied by a signed agreement that includes a non‑endorsement clause.
- The project does not accept funding from entities whose primary business is fossil fuels or surveillance software (as defined by the Core Team’s ethical funding policy).

## Governance alignment
The Core Team is responsible for approving all funding agreements above $50,000. A public funding register is maintained in the repository at `/FINANCES.md`. The project publishes quarterly financial statements.

## Branding rules
- Funding sources may not use the ApexOS logo without explicit permission.
- Grantors may state “Supported by ApexOS” only if they have provided unrestricted funds.
- Merchandise vendors must sign a brand license agreement.

## Public recognition rules
- A “Funding Sources” page lists all entities that contributed $5,000+ in the last 12 months.
- Grantors are thanked in the README’s “Acknowledgments” section.
- Individual donors under $1,000 are not individually listed (aggregated as “Community Donors”).

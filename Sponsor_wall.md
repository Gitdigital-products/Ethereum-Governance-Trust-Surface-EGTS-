# Sponsor Wall

## Purpose
This document defines the “Sponsor Wall” — the primary public recognition surface for organizational sponsors of the ApexOS project. It includes visual logos, tier grouping, and linking rules.

## Definitions
- **Sponsor Wall**: The dedicated webpage at `/sponsors` showing all active sponsors.
- **Hero Section**: Top part of the wall reserved for Platinum sponsors.
- **Alumni Section**: Lower section for past sponsors (no logos, only text names).

## Tiers (if applicable)
Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum as defined in sponsorship tier documents.

## Benefits
- Each sponsor gets a clickable logo linking to their website (nofollow attribute added).
- Platinum sponsors receive a 200x200 pixel logo size; Gold 150x150; Silver 100x100; Bronze 80x80.
- Sponsors may submit a 30‑second video testimonial that appears on hover (Gold+ only).

## Requirements
- Logos must be vector (SVG preferred) or high‑resolution PNG with transparent background.
- Sponsors must renew their logo submission annually; outdated logos are replaced.
- Sponsors may request logo removal at any time, but this terminates recognition benefits.

## Governance alignment
The Sponsor Wall is managed by the Core Team’s marketing subcommittee. Changes to the wall’s layout require a simple majority vote. The wall must remain accessible without login or paywall.

## Branding rules
- All logos are displayed with a grayscale filter by default; color appears on mouse hover (to maintain visual consistency).
- Sponsor names must be set in the project’s primary font (Inter).
- No animated GIFs or flashing elements allowed.

## Public recognition rules
- Sponsor Wall is updated within 48 hours of a new sponsorship agreement.
- A “New Sponsor” banner appears for the first 14 days on the wall.
- Sponsors who maintain Platinum status for 3 consecutive years get a “Hall of Fame” badge on the wall.

## Templates (if applicable)
- Logo submission form: https://apexos.org/sponsor-wall/submit
- Sponsor Wall layout CSS: `/assets/css/sponsor-wall.css` in the repository
